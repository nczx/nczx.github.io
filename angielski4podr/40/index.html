(To nie musi tak być. = Nie musimy pozwolić, żeby to się wydarzyło!)

(Musimy podjąć działanie. = Lepiej zróbmy coś jak najszybciej., Musimy coś zrobić.)

Zadanie 3

Modal verbs: present

Rule 1

We use must, have to and need to to express obligation. - We have to do something.

Rule 2

We use don't have to/need to and needn't to express a lack of obligation. - It needn't happen - we can stop it.

Rule 3

We use can't and mustn't to express prohibition. - We mustn't let this crazy plan go ahead. 

Rule 4

We use can, could and may to express permission. - The Residents Action Group can organise a demonstration. 

Rule 5

We use ought to, should(n't) and had better to give advice. - Someone ought to contact the newspapers.

Rule 6

We use may, might and could to express possibility/probability. - This could be a huge disaster for our town.

Rule 7

We use can't and must for speculation/deduction. - The mayor can't be serious!

Rule 8

We use can and can't to talk about (in)ability. - I agree, but we can't stop them.

 

Modal verbs: past

Rule 9

We use must/can't/couldn't have + past participle to talk about certainty in the past. - The Residents Action Group must have won their campaign (...). 

Rule 10 

We use may/might/could have + past participle to talk about possibility in the past. - It might have been pressure from the mayor (...)

Rule 11

We use should(n't) have + past participle  to show regret for a past action. - Most residents say she shouldn't have backed the idea in the first place.

Rule 12

We use needn't have + past participle  to say that something that happened was unnecessary. - Luckily, we needn't have worried.

Uzasadnienie: wyrażenie prawdopodobieństwa - might/may.

Uzasadnienie: zakaz - can't/mustn't.

4. don't have to/don't need to/needn't give 

Uzasadnienie: wyrażenie braku przymusu - don't have to/don't need to/needn't. 

5. can't be

Uzasadnienie: spekulacje - can't. 

6. better call 

Uzasadnienie: dawanie rady - had better.
