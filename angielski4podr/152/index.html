Zadanie 1

Strony biernej (the passive) używamy, żeby położyć nacisk na czynność, a nie na jej wykonawcę. Tworzymy ją w następujący sposób:

czasownik be we właściwym czasie + past participle (+ by + wykonawca czynności);

present simple - is/are + past participle

present continuous - is/are being + past participle

present perfect - has/have been + past participle

past simple - was/were + past participle

past continuous - was/were being + past participle

past perfect - had been + past participle

future simple - will be + past participle

future continuous - will be being + past participle

future perfect - will have been + past participle

be going to - is/are going to be + past participle

 

1. was made

2. has already been downloaded 

3. had been left

4. will be released 

5. was being painted 

6. wouldn't be found. 

7. will have been completed


Zadanie 2

Kiedy chcemy opisać czynność, która została zlecona innej osobie, używamy konstrukcji have/get something done:

have/get w odpowiednim czasie + dopełnienie + past participle, np.:

I will have had my hair cut by the time you come back home.

(Do czasu aż wrócisz do domu, zetnę włosy.)

 

1. had my computer repaired/ got my computer repaired 

2. Have you had your hair cut 

3. is having his photo taken/ is getting his photo taken 

4. haven't had the house painted

5. to have your nails done/ to get your nails done

6. having my ears pierced/ getting my ears pierced

7. have never had my purse stolen

Zadanie 2

Zdania z czasownikami przechodnimi, które łączą się z dwoma dopełnieniami mogą być zapisane w stronie biernej na dwa sposoby. 

 

1. Some copies of the documents will be given to us. 

We will be given some copies of the documents. 

2. The final version should be shown to us tomorrow. 

We should be shown the final version tomorrow. 

3. An important letter has been sent to you.

You have been sent an important letter.

4. Good manners are being taught to us. 

We are being taught good manners.
