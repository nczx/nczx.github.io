Zadanie 3

1. You need to have an argument. 

2. You read different opinions about your topic and select the information that best supports your argument.

3. In the introduction state your argument and briefly outline what points you're going to make. Then, talk about each point fully and make use of supporting evidence and visuals. End with a conclusion. 

4. Make note cards and practice giving your presentation focusing on body language and the manner of speaking. Practice with a friend using the technology you are planning to use. 


Zadanie 4

Poniżej znajdują się techniki i przykłady wymienione w nagraniu. Pozostałą część zadania należy wykonać samodzielnie.

 

give an interesting fact

example: Did you know that the top of the Eiffel Tower leans away from the sun because the metal of one side heats up and expands?

begin with a story

example: On 25th of August 1875 Captain Matthew Webb became the first person to swim the English Channel between England and France. It took him 21 hours and 45 minutes. He became a national hero. 

set out the problem

example: The minute we uppload a photo of ourselves to a social media site, it's on the Internet forever and can be copied or saved by anyone. How can we protect ourselves against identity theft?

The list of does and don'ts for presentations

does:

  • make eye contact with people
  • stand up straight
  • speak clearly
  • vary your tone and pace 
  • smile at th audience

don'ts:

  • fold your arms
  • put your hands in your pockets
  • turn your back to the audience
